Improving Revenue Performance Through Reimbursement Analysis

Project Overview

Healthcare Operations Case Study

This project analyzes hospital encounter data to evaluate how different payers cover the cost of care. The analysis focuses on comparing payer performance based on the percentage of costs they reimburse. By examining payer behavior this project highlights patterns in healthcare spending and reveals where to focus efforts to increase reimbursements, thus decreasing patient burden.

The Problem

Healthcare organizations depend on timely and accurate reimbursement to maintain financial stability and support patient care. Variations in reimbursement performance across payers and service lines can create operational challenges and reduce revenue capture.

The goal of this project was to identify reimbursement trends, evaluate payer performance, and uncover opportunities to improve financial outcomes.

Why It Matters

Even small improvements in reimbursement efficiency can have significant financial impacts.

Understanding which payers, service types, and encounter categories contribute most to revenue performance helps organizations prioritize operational improvement efforts.

Data Considerations

The dataset included multiple tables requiring several joins to create a comprehensive reimbursement view.

In addition, some payers reported extremely low reimbursement values, making it important to distinguish meaningful trends from potential outliers and reporting inconsistencies.

Investigation

To evaluate reimbursement performance, I analyzed payer reimbursement data across encounter types, service categories, and insurance carriers.

The analysis focused on identifying high-performing and underperforming reimbursement segments while highlighting opportunities to improve financial performance.

Key Findings

  • Medicare generated the highest overall reimbursement volume.
  • Reimbursement performance varied significantly between insurance carriers.
  • Ambulatory services represented the largest share of billed encounters.
  • Urgent Care demonstrated the strongest reimbursement performance among service categories.
  • Several payers generated minimal reimbursement despite substantial claim volume.

Recommendations

Prioritize High-Volume Payers

Focus contract review and optimization efforts on payers responsible for the largest reimbursement volumes.

Investigate Low-Performing Carriers

Review reimbursement processes, denial rates, and contract terms for underperforming insurance providers.

Improve Documentation Workflows

Evaluate coding and documentation processes to maximize reimbursement opportunities across ambulatory services.

Potential Impacts

Improving reimbursement performance can increase revenue capture, reduce administrative burden, and strengthen organizational financial stability.

By focusing on high-impact payer relationships and operational processes, healthcare organizations can improve both efficiency and financial outcomes.

What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of evaluating operational performance from both a financial and process perspective. While identifying revenue trends is valuable, the greatest opportunities often emerge when financial findings are translated into actionable workflow improvements.
